About & Research

My research asks a simple question: do we really need huge image-based models to read emotions from faces? Instead of processing full video frames, I represent faces as graphs of facial landmarks and model their dynamics with spatio-temporal graph neural networks — relational convolutions, multi-branch fusion of landmark and muscle information, and learned graph topologies.

A systematic ablation of the whole pipeline across six benchmarks (DFEW, FERV39K, AFEW, MAFW, Oulu-CASIA, CK+) with subject-independent cross-validation revealed which components actually matter — inter-ocular normalization alone brings +18.4 points of accuracy, and a curated subset of 53 landmarks outperforms MediaPipe's full 468. The resulting models are light and interpretable: GradCAM-style node-importance analyses map directly onto FACS action units, offering an efficient alternative to large pre-trained image models.
